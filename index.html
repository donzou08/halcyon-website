Questions worth asking
Why a monthly retainer instead of a one time fee?

The system does not stop being useful the day it ships. Operations change, new problems show up, and a tool nobody maintains starts drifting from how the business actually works within months. The retainer covers that ongoing refinement. It is not a build and disappear engagement.

Who owns the software once it's built?

You get full, exclusive use of everything built for your business, permanently. Halcyon keeps the underlying codebase and architecture, the same way an engineering firm keeps its own tooling across projects. Your data and your system are yours to use without limitation.

How is this different from a freelancer or a large IT agency?

A freelancer finishes and moves to the next job, so maintenance becomes your problem. A large agency reuses the same template across clients because that is how they scale. Halcyon does neither. One person maps your actual workflow, builds specifically for it, and stays on to keep it working as the business changes.

How long until something is live, and what is a stabilization window?

Depends on scope, but the goal is always a working first version fast. Once a system goes live it gets a 15 working day stabilization window, the period where bugs and rough edges get sorted without it turning into new scope. Each system gets its own 15 day clock from its own go-live date. The retainer starts from the first system's delivery.

Do you disappear after launch?

No. Ongoing support, small enhancements, and fixes are part of the retainer, not billed separately. The point is the system keeps working as your operations evolve, not that you are handed something and left alone with it.

What does this cost?

It depends on scope, and there is no menu price because no two operations look the same. Engagements run as Starter, Growth, or Partner depending on how much of the business needs covering. Every one starts with a discovery conversation at no cost before anything is scoped or costed.

You have worked with one client. Why should I trust that?

Fair question. Two systems are live inside that business and a third is on the way, and every number on this site comes from that production data rather than a case study written after the fact. The methodology behind those figures is published on request, which is more than most firms with longer client lists will offer. References available on request.
